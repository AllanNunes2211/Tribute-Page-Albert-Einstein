Albert Einstein

The Genius Who Transformed Modern Physics

Here's a timeline of Albert Einstein's remarkable life:

  • 1879 - Born in Ulm, Germany, to Hermann and Pauline Einstein
  • 1895 - Renounces his German citizenship to avoid military service and moves to Switzerland
  • 1905 - Publishes his "Annus Mirabilis" papers, including the special theory of relativity and the famous equation E=mcÂ²
  • 1909 - Becomes a professor at the University of Zurich
  • 1914 - Moves to Berlin and becomes a member of the Prussian Academy of Sciences
  • 1915 - Completes the general theory of relativity, revolutionizing our understanding of gravity
  • 1921 - Awarded the Nobel Prize in Physics for his work on the photoelectric effect
  • 1933 - Emigrates to the United States to escape Nazi persecution, settling in Princeton, New Jersey
  • 1939 - Writes a letter to President Roosevelt warning about the potential development of "extremely powerful bombs of a new type" - which leads to the Manhattan Project
  • 1940 - Becomes a United States citizen
  • 1952 - Is offered the presidency of Israel, which he declines
  • 1955 - Continues his work for peace and scientific understanding until his death
  • 1955 - Dies in Princeton, New Jersey, leaving an unparalleled scientific legacy
